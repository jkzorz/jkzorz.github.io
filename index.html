Analyzing Microbial Ecology Data

I'm using these tutorials as a place for me to keep track of code I've used to generate R visualizations and statistical analyses, specifically for the purpose of visualizing and analyzing large microbial ecology datasets.

Disclaimer: I am in no way an R expert, but everything I use has basically worked for me once, and so I keep adapting it over time as needed. There are probably hundreds of better/different ways to do things in R than what I do, but all the code I'm sharing has worked for me and is what I've become relatively comfortable with.

I'm also not a stats expert by any means, and I rely a lot on some really great resources out there. My go to is GUSTAME, which is a guide to statistical analysis in microbial ecology. Also their papers:

  • Multivariate analyses in microbial ecology
  • A guide to statistical analysis in microbial ecology: a community-focused, living review of multivariate data analyses
